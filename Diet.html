Pumpkin Pie Protein Smoothie

We never met a pumpkin pie we didn't like.This smoothie makes your muscles stronger. It’s creamy, cool, includes all macronutrients (proteins, fats, and carbs) and it’s delicious. In addition, the pumpkin provides an amazing boost of nutrients.1 medium banana, peeled and frozen

1/2 cup canned pumpkin puree (not pumpkin pie filling)

1 ½ Tbsp. nut or seed butter of choice

1/2 cup unsweetened almond*, coconut milk or water

1/2 cup canned coconut milk or full-fat yogurt –> not dairy-free

1/2 tsp. pumpkin pie spice

¼ tsp. cinnamon

1 tsp. vanilla

2 scoops unflavored or vanilla protein powder of choice

2 tsp. maple syrup (optional-for a touch of sweetness) 1 cup ice

handful of spinach (optional)

Pumpkin is high in fiber, an excellent source of vitamin A & C, and antioxidant rich. This smoothie is low in sugar and can be made vegan or paleo friendly!

Instructions

Place all ingredients into the container of your blender.

Place the lid on top and blend until smooth.

Serve immediately or refrigerate for up to 24 hours.


Keto Smoothie

Raspberries, blackberries, and strawberries are lower in carbs than other popular smoothie fruits like bananas, pineapples, and mangoes. Add fat with coconut milk, and feel even better about your healthy breakfast with baby spinach (or kale!).

11/2 c. frozen raspberries, plus more for garnish (optional)1 1/2 c. frozen strawberries

frozen blackberries

coconut milk

baby spinach

Unsweetened shaved coconut, for garnish (optional)

instructions

In a blender, combine all ingredients (except for coconut). Blend until smooth. Divide between cups and top with raspberries and coconut, if using.

Healthy Diet

A healthy diet is a diet that helps to maintain or improve overall health. A healthy diet provides the body with essential nutrition: fluid, macronutrients, micronutrients, and adequate calories. For people who are healthy, a healthy diet is not complicated and contains mostly fruits, vegetables, and whole grains, and includes little to no processed food and sweetened beverages. The requirements for a healthy diet can be met from a variety of plant-based and animal-based foods, although a non-animal source of vitamin B12 is needed for those following a vegan diet. A healthy lifestyle may lower disease risks, such as obesity, heart disease, type 2 diabetes, hypertension and cancer.
